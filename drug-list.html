Top 10 most prescribed drugs and their uses

By Chibuzo Kelechi VP

July 27, 2023

Medicines/drugs have been with us since the civilization of man, originally in herbal forms and now in more synthetic modifications.

Till date, there are frequent, new discoveries in the field of pharmaceuticals to improve the standard of living, and also to cure (or maybe regulate) illnesses that were once thought to be incurable.

1. Atorvastatin

This drug tops the list, and it's also in the WHO's list of essential drugs. It was formulated by Pfizer inc.

Little wonder as it is used in the treatment of cardiovascular diseases(e.g. Heart attacks) and also patients with high cholesterol levels.
Some side effects include headaches, nausea, cold, etc.

2. Levothyroxine

Second in the list is Levothyroxine which is used to treat deficiency of the thyroid hormone, Thyroxine. Thyroxine controls weight gain and loss in the body, and also metabolism

Some side effects include nausea, cramps, palpitation, etc.

3. Omeprazole

This drug is used to treat ulcer and also excess acidity and flatulence in the stomach.
Some side effects include headaches, nausea, cold, etc.

4. Gabapentin

It is used as an anticonvulsant ,and in treatment or control of epileptic conditions

Some side effects include dizziness, fever. shallow breathing, et cetera

5. Antihistamines

A family of drugs for the treatment of and other symptoms like allergies and other symptoms like rashes and sneezing. It is generally used for the regulation of histamine(hormone released during allergic reaction).

Side effects include Drowsiness, headache, loss of muscle coordination.

6. Albuterol

This is for the treatment of breathing difficulties, coughing and most importantly asthma. It is usually used in nebulizers and inhalers


Side effects include headaches, nasal irritation, nervousness.

7. Antibiotics

First discovered in 1928 by Alexander Fleming, antibiotics have become one of the most popular drugs worldwide.

They are used to fight bacterial infections. However, they can't be used to combat viral infections like the flu.
Some side effects include Nausea, irregular heartbeat, blurred vision, etc.

8. Losartan

Used in the treatment of high blood pressure and it also lowers the risk of stroke.

It is, sometimes, used by patients of Type 2 diabetes and hypertension, for their kidney.
Its side effects are similar to those of the antibiotics.

9. Metformin

This drug is used in the treatment and management of type 2 diabetes. Works by lowering the blood glucose levels. It is recommended you take it with your meals.
Some side effects include discomfort, nausea, loss of appetite, et cetera

10. Hydrocodone

A special drug in the list, hydrocodone is used for cough and pain relief.
I said it's special cause it is addictive and is therefore used as a last resort. It acts directly on the pain center in the brain. Side effects, some of which appear on withdrawal include itching, mood swings, nausea, and addiction.

Paracetamol

Some of you might have wondered why it wasn't at the top of the list. Paracetamol(originally Acetaminophen), is a common household drug, especially in Nigeria.

It is used as pain relief.
People have been advised to minimize the intake due to its long term side effects, likeliver complications, among others.
